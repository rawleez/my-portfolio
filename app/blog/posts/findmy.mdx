# Find My +

## A Context-Aware Approach to Left Behind Notifications

## Problem

Apple's **Find My** helps users locate their devices and alerts them when they leave an item behind. However, today's notifications are based primarily on **location** rather than **context**.

For example, my iPad almost always stays at home. Yet every time I leave home, Find My reminds me that I left it behind. While the notification is technically correct, it isn't useful because the iPad was never expected to come with me.

Over time, repeated irrelevant notifications make it easier to ignore alerts that actually matter.

---

## Proposed Solution

Instead of only detecting whether a device has been left behind, Find My should learn which devices users typically bring to different destinations.

### Example

- When driving to work, I almost always bring my **MacBook** and **AirPods**.
- When going to the gym, I usually bring only my **AirPods**.
- My **iPad** almost never leaves home.

If I'm heading to work without my MacBook, Find My could notify me before I arrive.

If I leave my iPad at home, no notification is necessary because that matches my normal routine.

The goal is to notify users only when something they are **expected to bring** is missing.

---

## How It Could Work

Apple already collects many of the signals needed to support this experience on-device, including:

- Significant Locations
- Find My device locations
- Bluetooth proximity
- Apple Maps navigation
- Time of day
- Frequent destinations
- Siri Suggestions and Apple Intelligence

Rather than asking users to manually configure trusted locations, the system could learn their routines over time and adapt automatically.

---

## Additional Opportunity

The same behavioral model could also detect unusual situations.

For example, if my iPad has remained at home for months and suddenly travels with me to the airport, Find My could ask:

> **"Your iPad doesn't usually travel with you. Did you intend to bring it?"**

This helps detect unusual behavior instead of only detecting separation.

---

## Success Metrics

This feature could be evaluated using metrics such as:

- Increased engagement with Left Behind notifications
- Lower notification dismissal rates
- Fewer unnecessary notifications
- Higher user satisfaction with Find My

---

## Why This Matters

Find My already knows where my devices are.

The next step is helping it understand where those devices are supposed to be based on routines. By making notifications context-aware instead of location-aware, Find My could reduce notification fatigue while delivering more useful, personalized alerts—without requiring additional user setup.
