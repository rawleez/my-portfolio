# Watchtower

## Bringing operational visibility to frontline teams

## Problem

Frontline teams often rely on scattered spreadsheets, manual updates, and work experience to track equipment, compliance tasks, and accountability. That makes it difficult to answer simple questions like:

- Who owns this asset?
- Is it compliant?
- What changed this week?
- Where is the bottleneck?

For a small operations team, these gaps create unnecessary friction and slow down decisions.


## Approach

I built Watchtower as a lightweight operations visibility tool that centralized equipment tracking and reporting in a way that matched how the team already worked. The goal was to reduce manual overhead without introducing a heavy system that would be hard to adopt.

The experience focused on:

- clear accountability for each asset
- repeatable reporting workflows
- faster visibility into compliance status
- less time spent updating spreadsheets by hand


## Outcome

Watchtower helped the team move from reactive reporting to more consistent operational oversight. The system made it easier to see what was happening across the organization and reduced the burden of manual reporting on frontline managers.

The result was stronger visibility, better accountability, and more time for the team to focus on execution rather than coordination.
